    fiber internet faqs

    • Kinetic Fiber is an internet technology that transmits data by sending light waves along thin glass fibers. Traditional cable internet transmits data by sending electricity over copper wires. This allows fiber optics lines to send information faster and over longer distances resulting in higher speeds and greater bandwidth.

    • Typically, upload speeds are much slower than download speeds. For example, a plan advertising cable internet for 200 Mbps may deliver upload speeds as low as 10 Mbps. Kinetic Fiber offers equally fast upload speeds and download speeds (also called ‘symmetrical speeds’). This means you can experience seamless video streams, better gaming with less lag, and more reliable feeds on security cameras and baby monitors.

    • With a fiber connection, your home internet will run more seamlessly across all your devices. Efficiently supporting multiple users on multiple devices, fiber internet assures you don’t stretch your bandwidth and risk unwanted buffering or lagging. With lightning-fast upload and download speeds, your household has the performance you need for streaming, gaming, remote work and more.

    • Kinetic’s 2 Gig internet speed can provide up to 2000 Mbps of speed. It’s delivered via fiber optic connection for ultimate performance across more connected devices than ever before.

    • Your Whole Home Wi-Fi Set Up includes installation of a Kinetic Gateway and Whole Home Wi-Fi equipment (if needed) by one of our friendly, local technicians. If needed, we will add a new phone jack and certify that your signal has been optimized, and your devices have been successfully connected to your new Wi-Fi network.

    Kinetic's multi-Gig Fiber upload speeds are faster than advertised cable upload speeds, excluding areas where cable providers offer symmetrical upload speeds for service tiers 1 Gig and above.
