Privacy Policy

Print

Last Updated: June 16, 2026

Infolinks Media, LLC (also known as Infolinks) is an advertising technology company that provides a variety of online advertising-related services. We respect all concerns about privacy and want you to be familiar with how we collect, use, and share information, and how you can exercise the privacy rights available to you.

This Privacy Policy covers the information that we may process:

  1. About End Users through our Ad Services and Technology; and
  2. About individuals who visit, use, or interact with the Infolinks Properties.

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom, please see Section 5 (GDPR and EEA/UK Data Protection Rights) for the full detail of your rights and our obligations under the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 and UK GDPR.

If you are a resident of California, please see Section 6 (Additional Information for California Residents) for disclosures required under California law.

SECTION 1 – WHO WE ARE

Infolinks provides advertising automation technology via our supply side platform (the Platform) and related advertising tools (collectively, the Ad Services). We provide our services and tools to our Clients, which include Publisher Clients who own and operate websites, mobile applications, and other online services (each a Digital Property), and Media Buyers who are advertisers and other ad partners looking to distribute ad content through our Platform to our Publisher Clients. Our Ad Services help Clients display relevant online advertising to end users (End Users) who interact with Digital Properties that use our Ad Services.

Infolinks also collects information from individuals when they visit, use, or interact with: (i) www.infolinks.com and other websites operated by us, including client interfaces (the Websites); (ii) our software applications (the Apps); (iii) our social media pages (Social Media Pages); and (iv) our sales and marketing activities (collectively, the Infolinks Properties).

Data Controller: For the purposes of EU and UK data protection legislation, Infolinks Media, LLC is the controller of your personal information. Our registered address is:

SECTION 2 – PRIVACY FOR OUR AD SERVICES

This Section describes how we collect and use information we receive from End Users of Digital Properties that use our Ad Services.

2.1 What Information Do We Collect?

Information we automatically collect

When End Users visit a Digital Property that uses our technology, we (and our partners or vendors) use and deploy tracking technologies like cookies to automatically collect certain information about End Users and their devices. Some of this information (including unique identifiers stored in a cookie or mobile advertising IDs) may identify a computer or device and may be personal data in some jurisdictions, including the EU. However, our Ad Services are designed to process information in such a manner that it cannot be directly attributed to a specific, identifiable individual without additional information.

The information we may collect includes:

  • Browser and Device information, such as device type and model, manufacturer, operating system type and version, web browser type and version, user-agent, carrier name, time zone, network connection type, IP address, general location inferred from IP address, and identifiers assigned to a device (such as iOS IDFA, Android/Google Advertising ID, or other unique device identifiers including those stored in a cookie or browser cache).
  • Information about an End User’s behavior on Publisher Clients’ Digital Properties, such as activities or actions on those properties, session start/stop time, and geolocation (including latitude and longitude coordinates, only where the Publisher Client’s Digital Property has enabled location services and the End User has granted permission for advertising purposes).
  • Information about ads served, viewed, or clicked on, such as ad type, placement, click status, frequency of exposure, and whether the End User visited the advertiser’s website or installed an advertised product or service.

Information we receive from third parties

We may also combine, merge, and/or enhance the information we collect about an End User with information collected from our Publisher Clients and third parties (such as data providers). Publisher Clients may pass information to us directly through our Ad Services or via APIs when our Ad Services connect to their systems.

2.2 How Do We Use Your Information?

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Serving Ads: To allow Publisher Clients to offer advertising inventory and Media Buyers to bid on and fill that inventory with relevant ads.
  • Ad Reporting and Conversions: To provide reports to Media Buyers about when and how End Users have been exposed to, clicked on, or interacted with their ads.
  • Fraud Detection and Prevention: To identify invalid ad impressions, clicks, installs, or ad queries, and protect us and our Publisher Clients from fraudulent behavior.
  • Frequency Capping: To prevent End Users from seeing the same ad too many times.
  • Customizing Ads: To infer End Users’ likely commercial interests based on their activities across websites, mobile apps, and Digital Properties over time, and to supplement this with third-party data to customize and optimize ad delivery.
  • Performance Analytics: To analyze ad performance including views, click-through rates, and app installs.
  • Contextual-Based Advertising: To serve contextually relevant ads based on End User activity and inferred commercial interests.
  • Location-Based Advertising: To deliver location-based advertising using device location where permitted by law.
  • Improving Ad Services: To facilitate navigation, personalize the experience, audit, research, and improve our Ad Services, develop new services, and ensure our technologies function properly.
  • Legal Compliance: As necessary or appropriate under applicable law, to comply with legal process, respond to government authority requests, enforce our terms, protect our rights and operations, and pursue available remedies.

2.3 Cookies and Similar Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and other similar tracking technologies (such as pixel tags and web or browser caches) to collect information automatically from End Users’ devices. They help us identify unique browsers or devices and perform functions like limiting ad repetition, ensuring proper ad display, and serving ads to End Users.

For full details of the cookies we use, including how to manage or opt out of them, please see Section 7 (Cookie Policy) below.

SECTION 3 – PRIVACY FOR THE INFOLINKS PROPERTIES

This Section describes how we collect and use information when visitors interact with or use any of the Infolinks Properties (such as our Websites).

3.1 What Information Do We Collect?

Information that individuals provide voluntarily

Certain parts of the Infolinks Properties may ask visitors to provide personal information voluntarily, for example when a visitor registers for an Infolinks publisher or media buyer account, becomes an Infolinks customer, expresses interest in our products and services, subscribes to our marketing, or otherwise contacts us. The personal information we collect may include contact information (such as name, address, telephone number, or email address), contact preferences, and the nature of an individual’s request.

Information we or our third-party partners collect automatically

When using the Infolinks Properties, we or our third-party partners may automatically collect certain information from an individual’s device. In some countries, including those in the EU, this information may be considered personal data under applicable data protection laws. Specifically, the information we collect automatically may include IP address, operating system type and version, browser type and version, cookie ID, activities on the Infolinks Properties, and other information about the individual’s system, connection, and how they interact with the Infolinks Properties and other websites.

3.2 How Do We Use Your Information?

We may use information collected from the Infolinks Properties for the following purposes:

  • Marketing and Promotional Communications: To send marketing communications in accordance with an individual’s marketing preferences. You may opt out at any time.
  • Administrative Information: To send information regarding our services and changes to our terms, reports, conditions, and policies.
  • Processing Transactions: To process your transactions and for billing purposes.
  • Testimonials: We may post testimonials on the Infolinks Properties that contain personal information. By becoming a customer, you provide automatic consent to use your name and testimonial. To update or delete your testimonial, contact privacy@infolinks.com.
  • Contextual Advertising and Analytics: To engage in analysis, auditing, research, and reporting, and to deliver advertising based on your activity on the Infolinks Properties and other websites over time.
  • Account Management and Customer Support: To create or administer Infolinks accounts and provide customer support or other requested services.
  • Content Improvement: To help improve the Infolinks Properties and Ad Services and ensure content is presented effectively on your device.
  • Legal Purposes: As necessary or appropriate under applicable law, to comply with legal process, enforce our terms, protect our rights and operations, and pursue available remedies.
  • Business Purposes: For data analysis, audits, identifying usage trends, determining the effectiveness of promotional campaigns, and evaluating and improving our products, services, marketing, and client relationships.

3.3 Cookies and Similar Tracking Technologies

We and our third-party partners may use cookies and similar tracking technologies on the Infolinks Properties to collect and use information about visitors. Please see Section 7 (Cookie Policy) for full details.

3.4 Use of Infolinks Properties by Minors

The Infolinks Properties are not intended for nor directed to individuals deemed to be children under applicable data protection or privacy laws. We request that such individuals do not provide information through any of the Infolinks Properties. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under the age of 16. SECTION 5 – GDPR AND EEA/UK DATA PROTECTION RIGHTS

This Section applies to residents of the European Economic Area (EEA) and United Kingdom (UK). Infolinks Media, LLC acts as a Data Controller with respect to personal data collected through its platform and services, and has adjusted its technology and policies to fully comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) and UK GDPR.

5.1 Legal Basis for Processing

Our legal basis for collecting and using personal information will depend on the personal information concerned and the specific context in which we collect it. We rely on the following legal bases under Article 6 GDPR:

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): For the use of non-essential cookies and interest-based advertising on publisher sites. You may withdraw consent at any time via our Consent Management Platform (CMP) without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Legitimate Interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): For fraud prevention, security, analytics, improving our services, and certain advertising purposes, where such interests are not overridden by your rights and freedoms.
  • Contractual Necessity (Art. 6(1)(b)): To perform our contractual obligations with publishers and advertisers
  • Legal Obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): To comply with applicable laws and regulatory requirements.

Where we rely on legitimate interests to process your personal information, those interests include the purposes described in Sections 2 and 3 above. If you have questions about the specific legal basis we rely on, please contact us at privacy@infolinks.com.

5.2 No-Consent Default for EEA/UK End Users

Infolinks defaults to a No-Consent interaction when it is not possible to determine consent from an EEA or UK End User. Under this default, we will:

  • Mask location by removing the last octet from the IP address of all EEA End Users to de-identify such information and mask latitude/longitude.
  • Apply no cookie syncing or cookie targeting unless compliant consent has been obtained.
  • Pass a digital No-Consent notification to all advertising partners by default.
  • Ensure any vendor activated on our publishers has appropriate compliance.
  • Work with publishers to receive valid user consent where required.

5.3 Your EEA/UK Data Subject Rights

Residents of the EEA and UK have the following rights under the GDPR:

  • Right of Access (Art. 15): Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification (Art. 16): Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure (Art. 17): Request deletion of your personal data (right to be forgotten).
  • Right to Restriction of Processing (Art. 18): Request that we restrict the processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability (Art. 20): Receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object (Art. 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes.
  • Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Art. 22): Not be subject to solely automated decisions that significantly affect you without human intervention.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

Please note that because most of the information we store can only identify a particular browser or device and cannot directly identify you personally, you may need to provide us with additional information to enable us to identify the information we hold about you. Please see Section 8 (How to Make a Data Subject Rights Request) below.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. For more information, please contact your local supervisory authority.

SECTION 7 – WEBSITE AND PLATFORM COOKIE POLICY

This Cookie Policy describes the different types of cookies that may be applied on the equipment of consumers who visit Infolinks.com and any other websites owned and operated by Infolinks, Inc. and its affiliates (collectively, Infolinks).

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes to the cookies we use or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. Please re-visit this Cookie Policy regularly to stay informed. If you have any queries, please contact us at privacy@infolinks.com.

7.1 What Is a Cookie?

Cookies are a standard feature of websites that allow us to collect certain information from your browser or device when you visit our Sites. In relation to the use of cookies by our partners and clients, note that we do not have access or control over these cookies.

7.2 What Cookies Do We Use?

We use a variety of cookies on the Infolinks Sites and Platform for several reasons. We also may use weblogs, pixel tags, or web beacons, which are pieces of code that may be used to read and place cookies on a web browser, to collect information about how you interact with websites or advertisements.

Essential Cookies

We use cookies that are essential to making the Sites work. These enable you to move around the Sites and use their features, such as recording a user login, managing user sessions, authenticating users, or adjusting settings and functionality. Without these cookies, we may not be able to provide services necessary to use the Sites.

Preference Cookies

We use cookies that collect information about your choices and preferences, allowing us to remember language or other local settings and customize the Sites accordingly.

Social Media Cookies

Some pages on the Sites contain tools or applications linked to third-party social media service providers such as Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn. The social media service provider may set its own cookies on your device. You should check the relevant provider’s website for further details about how they use cookies.

Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect information about how people use the Sites, such as which pages are visited most often and how visitors move between webpages. These cookies provide analytical information about how the Sites are performing and how we can improve them. Examples include Google Analytics and Salesforce.

Advertising Cookies

We may partner with certain third parties to deliver advertising that we believe may interest you based on your activity on our Sites and other websites over time. These third parties may set and access cookies on your device and may also use weblogs, pixel tags, or web beacons. Examples include Quantcast and others.

7.3 How Do I Manage Cookies?

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. In addition to the options provided above, you may refuse or accept cookies from the Sites at any time by activating settings on your browser. Information about the procedure to enable or disable cookies can be found on your Internet browser provider’s website. You may also refer to http://www.allaboutcookies.org/manage-cookies/index.html for information on commonly used browsers. Please be aware that if cookies are disabled, some website features may not operate as intended.

For EEA and UK users, you may also manage your consent through the Consent Management Platform (CMP) displayed on publisher sites using Infolinks technology, or via our opt-out page click here.

SECTION 8 – HOW TO MAKE A DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS REQUEST

If you wish to make a data subject rights request, please follow the steps below.

Step 1: Locate Your Digital Identifiers

In order for us to locate relevant records, we will need you to provide us with your Digital Identifiers. You can find your Digital Identifiers as follows:

Web Browsing

Our technology operates by using text files called cookies that are placed on your device. Some of the cookies we use include a Digital Identifier. To locate these Digital Identifiers, search through your cookies for cookies named in our Cookie Policy (set from the Infolinks domain). The text in each of these cookies is a separate Digital Identifier. Please keep in mind that different cookies are placed on each browser and device you use to access the Internet.

Mobile Devices

Certain mobile devices generate a persistent Advertising Identifier per device. On iOS devices, this may be referred to as an IDFA, IFA, or ID for Advertising. On Android devices, it may be referred to as an Advertising ID. Please follow instructions from your mobile device manufacturer on how to locate your specific Advertising Identifier.

Step 2: Verify Your Digital Identifiers

To ensure we respond only to the person authorized to make the request, we also need supporting proof that you are connected to the Digital Identifiers located in Step 1. For Digital Identifiers stored in cookies, please take a screenshot showing both the cookie name and the full Digital Identifier. For mobile identifiers, please take a screenshot displaying the Advertising Identifier.

Additionally, please complete, sign, and include the Infolinks Data Authorization Form in Section 9 below. By signing, you declare under penalty of perjury that all statements are true, and a witness must also sign and date the form. Both you and the witness must each provide a copy of one identifying document such as a passport or driver’s license.

Step 3: Contact Us

Once you have located your Digital Identifiers and can provide verification, please contact us at privacy@infolinks.com with:

  • Your full name
  • The country in which you are located at the time of the request
  • The data subject rights you wish to exercise
  • A full list of your Digital Identifiers
  • Supporting proof as described in Step 2
  • A signed Authorization Verification Form with witness signature
  • The identity documents described in Step 2

You can also make a data subject rights request by postal mail, sending a written request with all of the above information to:

Infolinks Media, LLC c/o Privacy 45 Broad Street, Second Floor Ridgewood, New Jersey 07450, USA

We will respond to your request within 30 days. We have a responsibility to verify your identity before responding, and we commit to not accepting requests submitted by third parties including automated robots.
