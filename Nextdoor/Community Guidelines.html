Nextdoor is built on the idea of creating stronger, safer, and happier neighbourhoods. These guidelines are designed to help foster a positive and trustworthy environment for everyone. By using Nextdoor, you agree to abide by these rules, which are enforced by Nextdoor to ensure a safe and respectful community.

1. Be respectful to your neighbours

Remember that youre interacting with real people - people who might live nearby that you may see out and about in your neighbourhood. 

  • Be civil and respectful: Disagreements are normal, but personal attacks, harassment, bullying, or belittling others is not tolerated. Focus on the issue, not the person.

  • Engage in good faith: Contribute constructively to discussions. Avoid trolling, inflammatory remarks, or provoking arguments.

  • No public shaming: Do not use Nextdoor to shame, humiliate, or single out individuals, even if you disagree with them. If you have a negative experience with a local business, share it respectfully and factually.

2. Do not discriminate 

All neighbours are welcome on Nextdoor. We have a zero-tolerance policy for content promoting discrimination, hatred, or violence against individuals or groups based on characteristics such as race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, disability, gender, sexual orientation, or age.

  • Respect diverse backgrounds: Our neighbourhoods are diverse. Embrace and respect the differences in your community.

  • Report harmful content: If you see content that violates this rule, report it immediately.

Note: You can also use this form to report harmful activity

3. Discuss important topics in the right place 

Nextdoors primary purpose is to help neighbours connect with whats happening locally. For broader discussions, like national or international topics, connect with interested neighbours using our Groups feature.

  • Focus on local matters: Share information, ask questions, and discuss topics that directly impact you, your neighbourhood, or local area.

  • Use Groups for broader topics: For discussions on national politics, religion, or other non-local issues, use or create a dedicated neighbour-created group. These groups create a space for interested neighbours who want to engage in these conversations. 

  • No political campaigning: Do not use Nextdoor to promote political campaigns, candidates, or fundraising. Local political discussions that impact a neighbourhood are permissible as long as they remain civil and respectful.

4. Use your true identity

Nextdoor is an online space for neighbourhood conversations - the kind you'd have in person.  We ask everyone to use their real name and identity, helping neighbours know exactly whos part of the conversation. Maintaining authenticity is crucial: impersonating others or creating fake accounts is prohibited.

5. Do not engage in harmful or illegal activity

Nextdoor is committed to maintaining a safe, welcoming space for neighbours to connect. We prohibit activity that could harm others - whether its physical harm, scams, or anything that puts neighbours at risk. We also strictly ban any content or behaviour that violates UK law or the laws of any country where Nextdoor operates. Here's what we don't allow:

Prohibited content

 

  • Threats to safety: Content that promotes violence, self-harm, or dangerous activities has no place in our neighbourhood community.
  • Illegal transactions & scams: Using Nextdoor to facilitate illegal transactions, run scams, or commit fraud. If you spot suspicious behaviour, please report it to help protect your neighbours.
  • Privacy violations: While we require real identities to build trust, never share others' private information without their permission.
  • Spam & commercial misuse: Excessive self-promotion and irrelevant advertising disrupts genuine neighbourhood conversations. We recommend using designated features like For Sale & Free or Business Pages for self promotion.

 

Illegal content

We have zero tolerance for content that's illegal under the laws of countries where Nextdoor operates, including:

  • Child abuse or exploitation material

  • Content facilitating human trafficking

  • Terrorist content or materials

  • Hate crimes or incitement to violence

  • Harassment, stalking, or cyberbullying

  • Fraudulent schemes or financial scams

  • Content promoting self-harm or suicide

  • Graphic violence or sexually explicit content

  • Sale of illegal drugs, weapons, or controlled substances

  • Content facilitating serious criminal offences

  • Unwanted messages with romantic or flirtatious intent as well as non-consensual intimate image sharing

Our response: When illegal content is reported, it will be removed inline with Nextdoors Community Guidelines and result in account restriction. This helps ensure Nextdoor remains a trusted space where neighbours can connect safely.

Report concerns: See something that doesn't belong? Use our reporting tools to help keep your community safe and welcoming for everyone.

Maintain community integrity

Help us keep Nextdoor a reliable and authentic platform for neighbourhood connections.

  • Respect intellectual property: Do not post content that infringes on copyrights, trademarks, or other intellectual property rights.

  • Authentic content: Post genuine content and do not engage in manipulation of the platform (e.g., creating multiple accounts to artificially inflate engagement).

  • Report misconduct: If you witness a violation of these guidelines, please report the content or profile. Your reports help us maintain a safe community for everyone. 

Moderation and Enforcement 

Nextdoor uses a blended balance of both technology and human review to detect and take action on content that violates our Community Guidelines and Member Agreement, including:

  • Algorithms to proactively detect harmful content

  • Product features that enable all neighbours to report both hurtful and harmful content and people, and distribute to the appropriate teams for review 

    • More information on our reporting process here

  • Community reviewers and a team of trained Nextdoor employees who review content and profiles that have been flagged, and take appropriate action to support the neighbours involved. Learn more about our Nextdoor Content Moderation.

Appeals process

Moderating online conversations is often subjective, and requires context about the relationships between people involved. We understand that mistakes can be made. Content authors have the opportunity to appeal a content removal or account restriction decision if they feel it has been made in error. Read more about;

  • Appealing content removal decisions

  • Appealing account restriction decisions

We believe that by adhering to these guidelines, we can collectively build a more connected, helpful, and vibrant Nextdoor for every neighbourhood.

Manage your Nextdoor experience 

We are committed to effective moderation and will remove any content  that violates our platform rules. For content that complies with our Community Guidelines that youd rather not see, we offer tools that help neighbours customise their experience. You can:

 

  • mute or block any neighbours content you prefer to avoid 

  • hide specific posts from your feed
