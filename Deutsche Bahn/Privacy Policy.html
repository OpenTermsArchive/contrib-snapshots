Privacy statement for the DB customer accounts

All information

 

The privacy statement for DB customer accounts supplements the general bahn.de/int.bahn.de privacy statement.

This statement describes what personal data we process in connection with DB customer accounts and how you can opt out of this data processing.

Detailed information

We collect and process your DB customer account data exclusively for specific purposes. These may arise due to technical necessity, contractual obligations or express requests on the part of users.

Processing personal data in connection with your DB customer account is based on Article 6 (1) (b) GDPR. This also applies to processing that is required in order to take steps prior to entering into the contract, e.g. in cases of inquiries regarding our products or services.

Insofar that we obtain your consent for the processing of personal data (i.e. if you subscribe to our newsletter or use the remember me option), this consent shall serve as the legal basis according to Article 6 (1) (a) GDPR.

If we are subject to a legal obligation that requires us to process personal data, for example to fulfil tax obligations, this processing shall be based on Article 6(1)(c)) GDPR.

We would like to use your previous and current usage patterns regarding your customer account to provide you with customised contents that will make our range of products more interesting to you. To do so, we store and analyse pseudonymised usage data from online activities. We can then offer you special advantages such as ticket price reductions and free seat reservations the next time you book a ticket. The legal basis for this is Article 6 (1) (f) GDPR.

The following contains a more detailed description of the data processing activities that take place when you register for and use a DB customer account.

List of specific examples:

Creating a DB customer account
The following mandatory information is required when users create a DB customer account:

  • User name (e-mail address) and password

It is not possible to create a personal account without supplying this information. The provision of further information about you and your travel profile is voluntary, or may be required if you wish to use certain customer account functions, such as providing your first name and last name to book tickets or other data necessary for controlling for any additional apps and services you have linked to your DB customer account. We store your booking and login data in your customer account and use it for performing internal analyses and market research.

Additional login function using passkey
You can set up passkeys for secure and fast access to your DB customer account on devices like smartphones that provide secure login procedures, e.g. by fingerprint or facial recognition. Only create passkeys on private devices that you manage yourself. Please note that once a passkey has been created on your device, anyone who can unlock the device can log in to your DB customer account using this passkey. Please refer to the manual for your device or operating system to find out how to save the passkeys in your device.

Even after you have set up a passkey, you can still log in with your user name and password on the device in case you want to log in to a different customer account, e.g. a business account. 

In the Account Manager section of your customer account, you will find an overview of passkeys that have been set up for your customer account. You can also block passkeys so that a new user name and password are required on the associated device.

Two-factor authentication security function
To increase the security of your DB customer account against unauthorised access, you can set up two-factor authentication (2FA) in the account manager. This is based on an additional one-time password, which is generated every time you log in and must be entered in order to access the customer account. You can store a mobile phone number to which this one-time password is sent via SMS. Alternatively, you can set up an authentication app with which you can generate and display the one-time password, usually in the form of a 6-digit numerical code. 

Remember device function
To make two-factor authentication more convenient, you can activate our "Remember device" function when logging in with the one-time password. This uses a data packet/browser cookie (KC_2FA_TRUSTED_DEVICE) to identify the device used as a trusted device for the specified period. The two-factor authentication will be paused on this device during this period. It will be requested again after this period has expired, if the cookie has been deleted or after you have removed this device from the list of trusted devices in the account manager. You can have multiple trusted devices remembered at the same time. To make them easier to manage, each remembered device is listed in the account manager with an automatically generated name and date. You can customise this name however you like.

Notification of account activities via e-mail
In the event of certain changes to personal data in your DB customer account, e.g. the details of stored payment methods, you will receive an e-mail notification to inform you and let you take action if this change was unwanted. This notification service is provided for security reasons, has no advertising purposes and will be expanded further.

Booking a digital ticket
When booking digital tickets, address data as well as title, surname and first name are taken from the DB customer account. The legitimate interest in processing the form of address is to address customers politely. You have the choice between "Mrs", "Mr" or "neutral form of address" and therefore do not have to assign yourself to a gender. For technical reasons, this is still a mandatory field. When purchasing in staff-operated sales, the digital ticket can alternatively be issued as a printout without entering an e-mail address. When booking international tickets and some regional offers, the date of birth may also be required. If the required data was not already available in the customer account, it will be recorded for the first time when the booking is made and saved in the customer account. On the train, the information shown on the ticket (first name and surname) will be displayed on the control device during the inspection.

Buying BahnCards
When you purchase a BahnCard, our system uses the contact and identification data in your DB customer account. Further information on data processing in connection with the BahnCard can be found at: https://www.db-fernverkehr.com/datenschutzhinweise/datenschutz-bahncard (only in German)

Payment details in DB customer accounts
We process your payment details when handling transactions, such as when you buy a product via bahn.de/bahn.com. Depending on the payment method chosen, we may forward your payment details to a third party, as we work with partner companies in order to process certain payment options. You can use the payment services of PayPal, paydirekt and Klarna without payment details being stored in your customer account.

  • Registration for payment by SEPA direct debit
    When you register to use the SEPA direct debit process, you provide us with a SEPA mandate that we can use to deduct payments from your bank account by means of a SEPA direct debit if you have selected this payment option. The legal basis for this is Article 6 (1) (b) GDPR.
     
  • Online activation of the SEPA direct debit scheme 
    To ensure secure payment with the SEPA direct debit scheme, we provide methods for online verification of account access via OpenBanking through Tink Germany GmbH (Gottfried-Keller-Strasse 33, 81245 Munich) or Verimi GmbH (Oranienstrasse 91, 10969 Berlin), or for online identity verification through Verimi GmbH. Depending on which verification method you choose, your personal data (the bank details, name and e-mail address provided) will be transmitted to the service provider under your direction. You will be guided through the selected function and informed about each individual step of the data processing in the automatically opening dialogue of the service provider. Once you have successfully completed the check, you can pay by direct debit. Both service providers are independently active as controllers. Verimi GmbH will offer you the use of your Verimi customer account, if you have one, or let you create a new customer account that will later also assist you with other identity verification procedures. Tink Germany GmbH and Verimi GmbH are authorised account information services that also work for banks and only process your data for the few minutes it takes to perform the account access check. 
    Further information is also available in the privacy statement in the dialogue window of the respective provider.
     
  • Payment by credit card
    To ensure that your payments are processed securely, payment-related data (amount, booking reference, booking description, payer) in your DB customer account is forwarded to a payment service provider. The legal basis for this is Article 6 (1) (b) GDPR. Our payment service provider for processing credit card payments is PAYONE GmbH, Lyoner Strasse 9, 60528 Frankfurt am Main, Germany. To learn how Payone processes your data, please read its privacy statement at https://www.payone.com/dsgvo/. The payment service provider performs the following: processing of credit card data in order to perform payments and store details in your customer account; application of security measures used by your card’s issuer (such as 3D Secure and strong authentication). No other institution handles your data. We do not receive access to your full credit card data. Instead, we merely save a reference in the form of an abbreviated credit card number so that you can identify it. To prevent cases of fraud, a data processor is used to process your device or browser fingerprint along with your payment-related data. This serves to protect you and us by preventing the misuse of your payment method when making payments via bahn.de/bahn.com. The legal basis for this is Article 6 (1) (f) GDPR.

Inquiries regarding bookings
If you use the contact form to ask questions regarding a booking, our system uses the details in your DB customer account, including the contact details it contains, to process your enquiry and in the event of follow-up questions.

Ordering subscriptions online
When you purchase a season ticket as a subscription, our system uses the contact and payment data in your DB customer account. Depending on the offer, identification data such as date of birth or a photograph may also be required. Your customer account displays your current subscription.

Newsletter registration
If you use your DB customer account to register for a newsletter, our system uses the relevant data in your account.
When you register for a newsletter, we also store the IP address assigned by the Internet Service Provider (ISP) to your end-user device used at the time of registration, as well as the date and time of registration. The collection of this data is necessary in order to trace (possible) subsequent misuse of the e-mail address of the person concerned and it therefore serves our legal protection. We want to be able to provide you with information that is relevant to you, so we analyse your interest in the contents of the newsletter based on clicks and the display of content via customised links. You may unsubscribe from the newsletter at any time by clicking the unsubscribe link at the bottom of the newsletter.

Participating in competitions
When we run competitions, we collect data for managing the process. The precise details, i.e. what data is collected and for what purpose, are available on the web page of the relevant competition.

More Information

Your DB customer account offers you secure access not only to bahn.de/int.bahn.de and DB Navigator, but also to various other mobility services.

In order for you to use compatible services with the DB customer account, it may be necessary for us to process additional personal data in your DB customer account. Your DB customer account remains your secure and central location for storing personal data and delivers to each mobility service only the data it requires for as long as the link between the service and your DB customer account exists. The scope of the data required for the service and the processing of your data by the service are set out in the user contract that you entered into with the provider of the service.  

Possible additional data required for apps and services
Depending on the functions offered by a service or product, one or several items of information may be required or can be added voluntarily: form of address, academic title, first name, last name, date of birth, street, house number, postcode, city, region, country, mobile phone number, e-mail address. 

Activation of apps and services
If you wish to use a provider's mobility service or digital product with your DB customer account, you will be informed about the specific personal data required and requested to add any data not yet saved in your customer account. We mark the data required by such a provider as mandatory information. You may choose voluntarily to add further optional data serving certain purposes where appropriate. Once you have logged in to your DB customer account, the service will be linked to your account.

Linked apps and services
Once you have linked your DB customer account to a service, the service provider will have access to the personal data from your DB customer account that it needs to fulfil the contract concluded with you and that you released for this purpose when activating the service.

In the profile area of your DB customer account, you will see the current overview of the linked apps and services, as well as the personal data stored and associated with the services, and you can make changes there pursuant to the rules of the DB customer account. The terms of use of the linked services may result in additional rules or restrictions regarding the changing of shared data. Any changes made to data will be automatically forwarded to the relevant service providers with whom you have logged in via your DB customer account. If you have activated several services, each service provider will only receive the updates for which you have granted authorisation. Data that is required for several services at the same time is updated simultaneously for all services. Depending on the service, the automated update is carried out in the background if possible or, if not, the next time you use the service or log in via your DB customer account.

For security reasons, a provider's service that is linked to your DB customer account can receive data from your DB customer account for a maximum of 180 days without any action on your part being required. After this security period has passed, data usage will only be re-enabled once you have actively logged in to your DB customer account and renewed the link to the service. The length of this period may vary from service to service. 

Deletion of apps and services
You can actively withdraw access to a service at any time in the settings of your DB customer account, thereby cancelling the link to the respective service. Please note that by doing so you may lose access to the service; it is therefore advisable that you log in to the respective service first and comply with any conditions of the service set out in the user contract that you entered into with the service provider. This may mean that the link between the DB customer account and the service must remain in place and cannot be cancelled until all the necessary conditions have been met.

The processing of the data in your DB customer account used to control apps and services, and the transfer of your data to the providers of the activated services take place strictly in accordance with Art. 6 (1) (b) GDPR. Data processing is necessary in order to fulfil the contract concluded between you and us, and the contract concluded between you and the provider, and also to have such a contract concluded at your request. 

More informations

Contract processing generally requires the involvement of data processors who are subject to our instructions, such as e.g. computer centre operators, printing or mail-order service providers, or other agents involved in contractual performance.

External service providers who process data on our behalf are carefully selected and placed under strict contractual obligations. The service providers work in accordance with our instructions and this is verified by technical and organisational actions and supplementary checks.

In addition, we only disclose your data if this is required by the linked apps and services, when you have given us your express consent or where we are under a statutory obligation to do so. Transmission to third countries outside the EU/EEA or to an international organisation will not take place unless we have been given reasonable guarantees. These include the EU standard contractual clauses and an adequacy decision by the EU Commission. For example, we may be required to forward data in the following circumstances for the purpose of contract processing when users book services on bahn.de/int.bahn.de:

  • Purchase of travel insurance
  • Purchase of hotel services
  • Use of the car rental service
  • When making use of services for travellers with reduced mobility, your data is sent to the appropriate offices of the DB Group departments involved.
  • Credit check when registering for the direct debit procedure by Experian Solutions GmbH
  • In the case of payment irregularities or payment default, details of the account receivable may be sent to a debt collection agency.

You purchase our partners' services on bahn.de/bahn.com directly from these partner companies. Further information on this is available under "Do you incorporate data from third parties?"

All informations

We only store your data for as long as necessary so we can achieve the purpose for which it was collected (e.g. in the context of a contractual relationship) or insofar as permitted by law. Thus, in the context of a contractual relationship, we will store your data at least until full and final completion of the contract. Thereafter, the data will be stored for the statutory storage period.

Your access to your DB customer account can be systematically deleted after 24 months of inactivity and the linked apps and services removed. The data in your customer account will also be deleted afterwards if there is no justified reason for retaining it.

All informations

Akamai Technologies GmbH
To protect your privacy and the data in your DB customer account from automated and malicious access attempts, we use security technologies from the provider Akamai Technologies GmbH (Parkring 22, 85748 Garching, Germany). Measurement results and connection parameters can be temporarily stored on your device for testing purposes, e.g. in cookies, for up to one month. The legal basis for the use of the technology is Art. 25 (2) 2 of the German Telecommunications and Digital Services Data Protection Act (Telekommunikation-Digitale-Dienste-Datenschutz-Gesetz "TTDSG") in conjunction with Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR. The legitimate interest is to protect the data of the data subjects and the infrastructure from automated and malicious access attempts.

CrossEngage
If you have a DB customer account, you can be shown personalised offers and promotions after you have logged in. If you have consented to the use of marketing cookies, your online usage behaviour will also be taken into account. Cookies with a duration of 13 months are set in your browser for the design of personalised content on bahn.de. The data used for this purpose is processed in pseudonymised form on the servers of our service provider CrossEngage GmbH (Bertha-Benz-Str. 5, 10557 Berlin). The legal basis for the use of the technology is § 25 para. 2 no. 2 TDDDG in conjunction with Art. 6 para. 1 lit. b) GDPR.
