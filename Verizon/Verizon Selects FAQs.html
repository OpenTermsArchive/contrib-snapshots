Verizon Selects FAQs General Information
  1. What is Verizon Selects?

    Verizon Selects helps make Verizon and third-party marketing you see more personalized and useful to you across the devices and services you use. This program shares information with Verizon Media.

    Verizon Selects uses:

    • Information about your wireless device and how you use it - including web addresses of the sites you visit, information about the apps on your device and features you use, as well as device and advertising identifiers.

    • Information about your device location including network data and location information transmitted by apps and sites you permit to use your device location.

    • Your postal and email addresses.

    • Information about your Verizon products and services and how you use them (such as data and calling features and use, phone numbers you call and receive calls from and the times you make and receive these calls, Fios service options, equipment and device types). Some of this information is CPNI (Customer Proprietary Network Information), which is information about the quantity, type, destination, location, and amount of use of your Verizon telecommunications and interconnected voice over internet protocol (VoIP) services and related billing information.

    • Information we get from other companies (such as gender, age range, interests, shopping preferences, and ad responses) or that you provide.

    • Information advertisers share with us to better target their own advertising.

    Most of this information may be combined with information collected by Verizon Media when you access their services and visit third-party websites and apps that include Verizon Media advertising services (including web browsing, app usage, and location information), as well as information we obtain from third-party partners and advertisers. This information is described in Verizon Media's privacy policy.

    Information about phone numbers you call or receive calls from won't be combined with Verizon Media information.

    The advertising program uses online and device identifiers including browser cookies, advertising IDs from Apple and Google, and one created by Verizon, known as a Unique Identifier Header or UIDH. Verizon includes a UIDH in the address information of Internet requests going to Verizon companies, including Verizon Media. When you join Verizon Selects, the UIDH may also be shared with partners who help us provide advertising services. Verizon partners are authorized to use the UIDH only as part of Verizon and Verizon Media services and not for their own separate uses. Learn more about UIDH.

    Review the full Verizon Selects Participation Agreement.

  2. If I participate in Verizon Selects, will you share data that identifies me with non-Verizon companies?

    We do not share information that identifies you as part of these programs other than with vendors and partners who do work for us and are required to protect the information and use it only for the services they are providing us.

  3. Will the Selects program use information obtained from my emails, texts or calls?

    The program may use de-identified information about Verizon Media services you use, including information from emails. You can read more in Verizon Media's privacy policy.

    The program also uses call information about phone numbers you call and receive calls from and times of your calls. This information is not used for third party advertising or personalization activities. It may only be used for Verizon advertising and personalization activities. The program does not access or use call content information.

    The Selects program does not use information obtained from texts.

  4. Where will I see advertising when I participate in Verizon Selects?

    You'll see ads in the same places you see them today, such as on websites and in apps on the devices you use or via mail, email or text when you have allowed it.

  5. What types of devices will receive advertisements when I participate in Verizon Selects?

    Devices you have on your Verizon wireless account and those you use to sign in to My Verizon will receive ads. In addition, you may receive advertising on devices you use to connect to Verizon Media sites and services, or on third-party sites where Verizon Media provides advertising services. If you share your Mobile Hotspot with others, ads relevant to you may also appear on other connected devices.

  6. What types of Verizon accounts may participate in Verizon Selects?

    Consumer and small business customers may participate in Verizon Selects. Corporate or government accounts aren't eligible to participate. Certain other lines may also be ineligible (e.g., lines that an Account Owner has requested be blocked from participation). If you share your Mobile Hotspot with others, data transmitted using that hotspot may also be used in Verizon Selects.

  7. How can I join Verizon Selects?

    You can learn more about how to participate by visiting the Verizon Selects Preference Center in My Verizon and following the prompts to opt in.

    If you agree to participate in Verizon Selects, you will also be included in Verizon's Business and Marketing Insights and Relevant Mobile Advertising programs.

  8. If I provided my consent to participate in Verizon Selects, and then I turn off the location-based services settings on my mobile device, will my location information still be collected and used for Verizon Selects?

    This program may use two types of location information. First, with your permission, it may use location information we collect from apps or mobile websites. This type of data will not be collected if you turn off the location service settings on your device. Selects also uses location information that Verizon collects from our network. This type of location information will continue to be used even if you turn off the location service settings on your device. If you do not want the advertising program to use your location information, you can withdraw your consent by visiting the Verizon Selects Preference Center.

    You can also instruct Verizon to stop using past data it has collected about your web browsing and location in the Verizon Selects program by visiting the Verizon Selects Preference Center. Information previously collected may continue to be used for analytics and modeling purposes.

    Verizon Media and Verizon also offer apps and other services that involve the collection of location information from your device (for example, MapQuest®). When you turn off location based services on your device or use other device controls, the ad programs will not receive location information from these apps. Verizon Media advertising services may also use location information provided by advertisers themselves. More details can be found in Verizon Media's Privacy Policy.

  9. If I agree to participate, how long will you keep and use my data for Verizon Selects?

    You will remain a participant in Verizon Selects until you withdraw your consent. Information used for Verizon Selects while you are a participant may be kept for up to three years.

  10. What kind of choices do I have as to whether my information is used for Verizon Selects?

    We won't use your information for Verizon Selects unless you provide your consent by opting in. Once you are participating in Verizon Selects, you can visit the Verizon Selects Preference Center to:

    • Instruct Verizon to stop using past data it has collected about your web browsing and locations to direct ads in the Verizon Selects program. Information previously collected may continue to be used for analytics and modeling purposes.

    • Disable other lines on your account from participating if you are the account owner or account manager.

    • Stop participating in Verizon Selects.

  11. How will I know that my Verizon Selects choices have been processed?

    You can view your current status in the Verizon Selects Preference Center.

  12. Do I have choices about being part of Verizon Media advertising services?

    You have choices about how Verizon Media uses your information for advertising purposes. You can opt-out of receiving interest-based ads when you browse the web by visiting the Digital Advertising Alliance's consumer choice page. This opt-out choice will apply only to the browser you are using when you opt-out and needs to be repeated if you’re using multiple browsers or devices. Blocking or deleting cookies in your browser may cancel your opt-out choices.

    You have additional choices about Verizon Media uses of data available at Verizon Media's privacy controls website.

    You can also opt-out of receiving targeted ads on your mobile device using the "Limit Ad Tracking" or similar capability on your device.

  13. Can I opt-out of Verizon advertising programs by deleting my browser cookies?

    No, using browser controls such as clearing cookies on your device or clearing your browser history is not an effective way to opt-out of the Verizon and Verizon Media advertising services. Instead, if you want to opt-out, you should take the steps outlined above.
