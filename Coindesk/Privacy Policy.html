1.Introduction

This Privacy Notice applies to all personal information processed by CoinDesk, including its affiliates and subsidiaries (“CoinDesk,” “we,” “us,” or “our”).

It covers the information CoinDesk collects through the websites, mobile applications, electronic devices, all other products and services we provide, any other services that display this Privacy Notice, all of the associated content, functionalities, and advertising, and when you communicate with us by phone, email, or otherwise (collectively, the “Services”).

This Privacy Notice governs the Services regardless of how you access them. Services include, without limitation, the following websites: coindesk.com; data.coindesk.com; cryptocompare.com; consensus.coindesk.com; consensus-hongkong.coindesk.com.

For CoinDesk Trading (powered by Bullish), please refer to the Privacy Notice available on the Bullish website.

This Privacy Notice does not apply to information that you may submit to third-party websites or mobile applications that may link to our websites or be linked to on our websites. We are not responsible for the actions or privacy practices of third-party websites and applications; please consult those websites and applications directly to understand their privacy practices.

By accessing and using our Services, you signify your acceptance to the terms of this Privacy Notice. If you do not agree with or are not comfortable with any aspect of this Privacy Notice, you should immediately discontinue access or use of our Services.

This Privacy Notice explains:

  • The information we collect about you;
  • The lawful basis for processing your information;
  • How we use the information we collect from and about you;
  • How we share your information with third parties;
  • What happens with linked sites, advertisements and embedded video player;

  • Cookies that we use or used by our service providers;
  • How we keep your personal information secure and the circumstances in which your information may be transferred to another country;

2. Information we collect about you

We collect, process, and retain information from you and any devices you may use when you use our Services.

2.1. Information you provide us

We may collect information about you during your use of the Services, including but not limited to: name, mailing address, phone number, email address, picture, date of birth, payment information, registration information, social media and messaging platform handle, optional biographic and demographic information, professional and licensure information, information for wallets you create or connect via our websites, survey responses, and any other information that you voluntarily provide. This includes information that you share with us on third-party websites and platforms.

If you apply for a job on our websites, we may collect your name, contact information, resume/CV and cover letter, and information from your LinkedIn profile or website.

2.2. Automatically collected information

2.2.1 Device information

We receive and store certain types of information about the computer or devices (including mobile devices) you use to access the Services. For example we may use cookies, tagging and other tracking technologies to help enable us to enhance or personalize your online experience. This information includes computer and connection information such as statistics on your page views, traffic to and from our websites, referral URL, ad data, your IP address, device identifiers, transaction history, and your web log information. We may also capture other data, such as search criteria and results.

2.2.2. Location information

We may collect location and device information when you use our Services. This includes general details like your IP address or ZIP code, and more specific data such as GPS functionality from mobile devices used to access the Services.

We use this location data to deliver location-based Services, including tailored advertising and personalized content. Additionally, if you scan one of our QR codes, we will identify the associated event, venue, or business, thereby collecting information about your location at the time of the scan.

2.2.3. Information from social media

When you interact with us or use our Services on a social media platform, we may collect the personal information that you make available to us, including your account ID, username, and other information included in your posts. If you choose to log into your account with or through a social networking service, we and that service may share certain information about you and your activities. When you give us permission, we may also collect information from your social media account on your behalf.

2.2.4. Information from other sources

We may supplement the information we collect with information offline or from third parties, in compliance with applicable data protection laws.

3. Lawful basis for processing your information

We only process personal information where we have a lawful basis for doing so, such as the following:

3.1. Your consent

We will process your personal information based on the permission you have given us for a specific purpose. For instance, if you submit a general enquiry form, we will ask for your consent if we intend to use your personal information for any other reason. You always have the right to revoke this consent by contacting us at: [email protected].

3.2. Legitimate business purposes

We may also process personal information when we have a legitimate business interest in doing so. For instance, if we become aware of copyright infringements while our Services are being used, identifying the responsible parties is our legitimate business interest. We take due care to balance our interests against your right to privacy.

3.3. Contractual necessity

This is where we have to process personal information to meet our contractual obligations. For example, if you want to attend a Consensus conference, we would need to process your billing information.

3.4. Legal obligation

This is where we have to process personal information in order to comply with the law. For example, we process and retain customer invoice information to comply with financial regulations.

We may also rely on relevant exemptions under the relevant data protection legislations to the requirement to identify a lawful basis for processing.

8. Cookies and tracking technologies

We use tracking technologies such as cookies, web beacons, and similar technologies to record your preferences, track the use of our websites, your interactions with our emails, and measure exposure to our online advertisements.

The cookies used on our websites and applications are divided into four general categories:

  • Necessary: cookies that are essential to make our websites usable by enabling basic functions like page navigation, access to secured areas of the website, and whether you have opted-out of certain non-necessary cookies.
  • Performance: cookies that help us understand how visitors interact with our websites by collecting and reporting about your device’s interactions.
  • Functional: cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalisation.
  • Marketing: used to track devices across websites to display ads that are relevant and engaging to our visitors to show you our ads on third-party websites.

We use third-party analytics services on our websites, including Google Analytics. These analytics service providers use cookies or other tracking technologies to help us analyze how users interact with and use our websites, compile reports about activity on websites, and provide us other services.

We work with third-party companies who also use these technologies to provide advertisements on our websites and other third-party websites. These third parties may use these technologies to collect information about you when you use our websites. They may collect information about your online activities over time and across different websites and other online services.

Some content, applications, and advertisements on our websites may be served by unaffiliated third parties. We neither have access to, nor does this Privacy Notice govern, the use of cookies or other tracking technologies that may be placed on your computer, mobile phone, or other device you use to access the Services by non-affiliated third-party ad technology, ad servers, ad networks, or any other non-affiliated third parties.

If you are a California resident, the CCPA also provides you with a right to opt-out of the sale and sharing of your personal information. The definitions of 'sell' and 'share' are extremely broad under the CCPA, and may include sharing certain pieces of information with our advertising partners, such as cookie identifiers, geolocation and interactions with advertisements, for the purposes of showing you advertising that is relevant to your interests.

You can choose to block sharing of this data with advertisers. This means that we turn off some types of advertising based on information you have given us and your use of our sites, ensuring that our advertising partners do not receive this data. By opting out, you will stop receiving adverts that are targeted specifically to you; however, you will still see the same number of adverts on our websites.

You can opt-out using the Cookies Settings link at the footer of the website you are using. Alternatively, you can opt-out by using a designated opt out preference signal.

We do not knowingly sell the personal information of consumers under 16 years of age.
